Block 4: Precision (& accuracy)

The distinction between “precision” and “accuracy” is a stated theme of many statistics courses. However, the vast majority of the techniques presented, e.g. confidence intervals, are only about precision. As for accuracy, the traditional course says two things:

  1. “sample randomly” and “assign treatments randomly.”
  2. Be aware how bias can creep into data collection and do your best to avoid it.

Since Block 3 covers regression and adjustment for covariates, QR2 has rather more to say about accuracy. We will return in Block 5 to another question of accuracy: how to represent hypothesized causal connections in the systems we model.
