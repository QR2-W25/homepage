Confounding and causality

We are thinking of intervening, e.g. buying new running shoes for an important race. We want to assess the likely implications for this intervention, which requires us to draw conclusions relating to causality.

  1. The intervention will correspond to our setting a causal explanatory variable, that is, a variable whose value we believe produces the desired output.
  2. We are interested in the effect size of that explanatory variable on the response variable.
